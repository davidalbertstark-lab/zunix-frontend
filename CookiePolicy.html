1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you use Zunix. They let us remember who you are, track what you do on the platform, and give you a smoother experience. Think of them like memory bookmarks - they help Zunix remember your vibe.

2. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to power the core of Zunix. Specifically, we use them to -

  • Keep your login session active
  • Secure your account and prevent abuse
  • Track your Engagement Score and platform interactions
  • Load your preferences like dark mode or saved settings
  • Analyze what features students use most
  • Optimize speed and performance
  • Personalize your homepage feed
  • Power features like missions, rewards, and dynamic leaderboards
  • Run experiments to test new features
  • Show brand-safe student promotions (if enabled)
No cookies - no vibe. Without them, Zunix won’t feel like Zunix.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies - Core for login, security, and navigation
  • Performance Cookies - Help us analyze usage and improve features
  • Functionality Cookies - Save preferences like theme and language
  • Targeting Cookies - Only used for student-safe campaigns (when active)

4. How Long Cookies Last

Some cookies expire when you log out or close your browser. Others last longer to keep your experience consistent. Duration varies from a few hours to a few months depending on the cookie type.

5. Third-Party Cookies

We use trusted third parties for analytics and testing -

  • Google Analytics for performance insights
  • A/B testing tools for experimenting with features
  • Campaign partners (only if you engage with them)

All partners must comply with privacy standards. No shady tracking, no data leaks.

6. Managing Your Cookie Choices

You can -

  • Block or delete cookies from your browser settings
  • Use our cookie banner to accept or reject non-essential cookies
  • Change your choices anytime from your profile or settings
Blocking core cookies may break features like login, score tracking, or filter memory.

7. Do Not Track (DNT)

We respect Do Not Track signals where possible. Some third-party tools may not fully honor it yet - we’re working on better compliance.

8. Future Updates

We may update this policy as Zunix evolves. When we do, we’ll change the effective date. If the changes are big, we’ll notify you on your dashboard.
